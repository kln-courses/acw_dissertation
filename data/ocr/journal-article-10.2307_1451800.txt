As is well known, a rational Bible exegesis began only with Saadya Gaon, whose activity was epoch-making in many respects. As far as our present knowledge goes, he wrote commentaries on the greater part of the biblical books, if not on the entire Bible.1 Nevertheless, Bible ex- egesis made no real strides until Hayyuj discovered the law of triliterality and the ingenious Abulwalid (R. Jonah) ibn Janal. continued his work and brought it to a certain degree of perfection. In a certain sense Abulwalid may be considered to have been a Bible exegete, since his works, and especially his dictionary (Kitab al- Usul), contain a considerable amount of exegetical material, out of which almost a complete commentary could be constructed ;2 nevertheless, Bible commentaries in the proper sense of the word were begun only by two of his followers, both of whom leaned on him and were active in Spain during the second half of the eleventh century, namely Moses ben Samuel Hakkohen ibn Chiquitilla and his younger contemporary and antagonist Abu Zakariya Yabya (Judah ben Samuel) ibn Bal'am of Toledo.
