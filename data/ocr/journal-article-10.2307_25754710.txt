Summary A new primate is described from middle Miocene coal-bearing beds at Trimmelkam (Oberosterreieh). It consists of the front part of the left mandible of a small anthropomorph, with two premolars and the first molar in situ. The impression of a very strong presumably 6 canine can be seen in the matrix. In addition a left medial incisor and a right lateral incisor of the mandible have been found isolated in the sediment. The following name is proposed for this new primate: Pliopithecus (Plesiopliopithecus) lockeri n. subgen. n. sp. The diagnosis of this new subgenus and the new species runs as follows: "An anthropomorph smaller than PL (Pliopithecus) antiquus (Blainville), but structurally related to the latter. The most conspicuous traits are: The heteromorph premolars of the mandible, i.e. the anterior premolar is longer than the posterior one. The sec tor ial shape of the anterior premolar is very striking. The outline of the corona seems to be elongated through the slanted position of the talonid which is pro truding lingually. The shape of the posterior premolar is oval, and possesses a trigonid pit that is closed towards mesial. The shape of the first lower molar is elongated in the mesio-distal direction. Its length-width-index is 78,3. The trigonid region appears to be clearly reduced as compared to the distal width of the tooth." ? The number of small anthropomorphs grouped around Pliopithecus is thus increased by this new form.
