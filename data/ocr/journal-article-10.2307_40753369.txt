In the first part of this book, the author engages in a two-tiered examination of nine nonver- bal elements integral to the rabbinic Amidah prayer: a detai- led historical-geographical consideration of their develop- ment, followed by an analysis of each gesture's signification, the crux of this study. Of all the possible models, it was the realm of interpersonal communication which had the stron- gest impact on this consideration of the rabbinic Amidah gesture system. The concluding chapters explore the broa- der rabbinic conception of prayer embodied in these nonver- bal modes of expression.
