<plain_text><page sequence="1">IASIL Bibliography Bulletin for 2007 On behalf of the IASIL Bibliography Committee, I am pleased to present the 2007 edition of the Bibliography Bulletin. Thanks to information provided by scholars throughout the world, and especially by representatives in many countries, an extensive list of entries is included in this edition. This is the second year in which the 'General Studies' section is divided into four sub-sections, making it easier, I hope, for anyone pursuing a line of research to find material. However, I have found that occasionally an item could be allocated to more than one sub- section, so readers should extend their search if necessary. The number of individual authors about whom research is being conducted continues to grow. As always in recent years, there are more items on James Joyce than on any other writer but it is interesting to note attention being given to less well-known writers, especially those from the distant past including the wonderfully-named Faithful Teate. Since the publication of the 2006 Bulletin, two new members have joined the committee: Karen Babine from USA and Noreen Doody from Ireland, both responsible for compiling a very large number of entries, so my thanks to them for undertaking the daunting task. I encourage everyone to send information about their recent publications to the country representative. I also welcome further members to the committee, to report especially from south-east Asian countries, South and Central America, Africa, and the Middle East. If you could represent a country at present without one, or you are aware of someone who could, do let me know at the email address below. And if you live in a country without a representative and are publishing on Irish literature, send me details before 31 May 2009. My thanks to the members of the Bibliography Committee, whose names are listed below, for their invaluable assistance in compiling this bibliography.
