<plain_text><page sequence="1">Der Drachentöter und das Menschenbild des Naturalismus"' Klaus Poenicke ABSTRAGT For Melville's Ahab, Moby-Dick reincarnates the dragon of ancient legend, the demon principle in life. Ahab assumes the equally traditional role of the dragon slayer, but perverts it, sacrificing the community instead of saving it. He fails through sensing a directional, inimical will where Melville progressively reveals a proto-naturalistic conception of indifferent force. In The Return of the Native, Hardy develops the theme. The Promethean fires in the opening scene destroy the identity of the dancers, instead of setting it off against the oppressive anonymity of the Heath. This anonymity no longer allows of mythic personification. Individual rebellion becomes absurd. For man, stoic endurance alone remains. With industrialism, the dragon reappears. In Zola's Germinal as in Norris' The Octopus, the machine becomes a canni- balistic ogre, calling up a new breed of dragon fighters. Where they fail, however, the life force itself moves in. Incarnate in the imagery of growth, it breaks the power of the monster. Assum- ing thus a quasi-ethical role, it creates a major temptation for the coming century. National, racial, social groups begin to claim specific covenants with the 'healthy', elementary forces of life. Thus authors like Zola and Norris become unwitting path-makers for some of the most militant ideologies, both leftist and fascist, of our own time. Mit dem Ausgang des Mittelalters verblaßt das Motiv des Drachenkampfes. Erst die Romantik mit ihrem wiedererwachten Mißtrauen gegen die Ratio, ihrem geschärften Bewußtsein von Mächten hinter den Erkenntnisbarrieren von Raum und Zeit, besinnt sich zurück auf die Fabelwelt des ,gotischenť Mittelalters und bereitet den Boden für neue Verbildlichungen des Dämonischen.
