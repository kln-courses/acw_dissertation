Robinson). - A new schematic representation of relationship reduces the contents of the whole family Hominidae to three genera only : Paranthropos, Australopithecus, and Homo. These genera are not of the same sort or of equal magnitude. Paranthropos and Australopithecus are validly distinct on any grounds. Both did exist synchronously in Africa over a substantial period of time, though at present they are not known to have been sympatric. Australopithecus and Homo are much more closely related and are probably two phases of the same phyletic sequence, though evidently one species of Australopithecus was contemporaneous for a short time with an early Homo erectus in the Sterkfontein Valley. There is no sharp discontinuity between Australopithecus and Homo - except in brain size in the known specimens. It is not a distinction of the same type as that between Paranthropos and Australopithecus or Paranthropos and Homo. This is just another of the numerous examples where the conventional Linnean taxonomic approach breaks down. This "vertical" type of arbitrary taxonomic distinction - as opposed to the "horizontal" taxonomy of contemp- oraneous forms - is one of the troublesome things which the paleontologist has learned to live with but which does not trouble the neozoologist. (The Smithsonian Report for 1961, pp. 479-500. Washington 1962.) Kenntnisse und Urteile über Pygmäen in Antike und Mittelalter (Martin Gusinde). - Der Ausdruck „Pygmäen" zur Bezeichnung zwerghafter menschlicher Mißbildungen kommt schon bei Homer vor, wobei er als ihre Heimat den Raum unweit der Nilquellen erwähnt. Die Neuzeit hat ihr dortiges Vorkommen als richtig bestätigt. Die alten Ägypter haben bereits Menschen von ungewöhnlichem Kleinwuchs als echte Rassenzwerge von Mißgestalten zu unterscheiden gewußt. Echte Rassenzwerge erfreuten sich hoher Wert- schätzung bei den vornehmen Klassen, während die Gruppe der Mißgestalten vorwiegend zur Belustigung der Volksmassen diente. Im Europa der ersten christlichen Jahrhunderte sind diese Kenntnisse fast vollständig verloren gegangen. Durch Aristoteles wurde Albertus Magnus wieder mit Pygmäen bekannt. Er stellte sie als Zwergmenschen zwi- schen vollwertige Menschen und richtige Affen. Petrus de Alvernia (f 1304) hat die Frage nach der Wesensart der Pygmäen neu gestellt, von denen er aus der Schilderung reisender Kaufleute aus Indien hörte, die Felle von Affen nach Europa gebracht und als „Pygmäen" zur Schau gestellt hatten. So ist die Ansicht der beiden genannten Scholastiker richtig gewesen : nach Albertus Magnus sind die afrikanischen Pygmäen nicht dem Tierreich zuzuweisen, und jene Wesen, von denen Petrus de Alvernia erfuhr, waren echte Affen aus Indien. Essai de méthode historique (Jan Vansina). - Quelle est la valeur historique qu'il convient d'attribuer à la tradition orale des peuples sans écriture ? Comme nous ne savons jamais pénétrer complètement dans la mentalité d'un autre ou dans une chose en dehors de nous, nous ne pouvons pas arriver à comprendre le passé. Mais en utilisant des techniques de probabilités, en interprétant et évaluant les données avec soin nous pouvons approcher la vérité historique même à défaut de sources écrites. Parmi les traditions orales celles qui sont le moins susceptibles d'être altérées sont les traditions figées et enseignées par des spécialistes dans les écoles, en outre celles qui font l'objet d'un droit de propriété, ou auxquelles une lourde sanction religieuse est attachée, ou encore celles qui sont transmises en utilisant des moyens mnémotechniques, comme par exemple des incisions dans un bâton. Pour juger la valeur historique d'une tradition orale il faut autant que possible éclaircir les circonstances du premier témoignage, com- parer les versions différentes et étudier les milieux de transmission. (Annales du Musée Royal de l'Afrique Centrale [Tervuren] Sciences Humaines n° 36, 1961, x-179 pp.) Les origines de la narration historique dans l'art du Proche-Orient ancien (Saba- tino Moscati). - L'élément historique occupe une large place dans l'art du Proche- Orient ancien; cependant, il s'agit dans la plupart des cas de représentations schématiques, sans individualité, et cette tendance est favorisée par des idées religieuses, surtout celle du roi-dieu. La représentation d'événements historiques concrets, narrative au sens propre du mot, ne se développe dans sa pleine vigueur qu'assez tard et dans une aire limitée, c'est-à-dire en Egypte du 14e au 12e siècle et en Assyrie du 9e au 7e siècle avant Jésus-Christ. En Syrie, Mésopotamie et Anatolie ce genre fait défaut, excepté des in- fluences égyptiennes et assyriennes. Ce genre pleinement historique dans l'art figuratif prend naissance en même temps que la grande politique internationale des empires et, dans le champ littéraire, le développement de l'annalistique ; il s'y fait jour une concep- tion plus « laïque » de la royauté qui insiste davantage sur le rôle du roi comme chef militaire et ses exploits dans leur situation concrète. La priorité en revient à l'Egypte dont l'art a influencé les régions de l'Asie antérieure ; puis, l'Assyrie a développé ces thèmes avec une vigueur et une originalité tout à fait spéciales. (Le origini delia narra- tiva storica neu' arte del Vicino Oriente antico. Atti délia Accademia Nazionale dei Lincei [Roma] Memorie, Serie VIII, vol. 10. 1961, pp. 51-99.) (Cahiers Alsaciens d'Archéologie, d'Art et d'Histoire [Strasbourg] 1961, 14 pp.) Trace of a Pagan Cult in the Cemetery at Sopronköhida (Gyula Török). - The cemetery of Sopronköhida (Hungary) supplied very valuable material regarding the transitory period in the worship of animal ancestors by the mountain peoples. Parts of the skulls of horned cattle were frequently observed on the decayed remains of the coffins or nearby and even a sheep skull fragment was found. From the animal skulls only the frontal bones with the horny parts were put into the grave while the rest had been re- moved by axe. From the finds we can conclude that the coffins were often covered with a blanket including the animal's head, i. e. the mask. Traces of this burial custom are also known from other sites. The animal masks represent the pagan animal ancestors who were thought to transmit their regenerative power to the dead through these images. Besides the animal masks, any sign, symbol, or even object representing the animal ancestor may give rise to the belief of having closer contact with the animal ancestor. The animal mask was also put on the sacrificial animals. (Folia Archaeologica [Budapest] 14. 1962, pp. 83-93.) Gypsy Medical Folklore in Hungary (Bêla Gunda). - Acculturation of gypsies in Hungary began early in the 19th century, and today no more nomadic gypsies do exist (while there are still many of them in Rumania). In Hungary, an increasing number of them are working in farms or in industrial plants and factories. Notwithstanding these changes in their mode of life, gypsy folklore in Hungary is still extraordinarily rich. The belief that illness is caused by evil spirits or by bewitching (especially by the evil eye) is widespread, and there are a great many of magical remedies for various kinds of sickness. The animals playing the most important role in gypsy medical treatment are the hedgehog and the snake. Especially high is the number of superstitions connected with the expectant mother and the newborn child. There are, however, also less conserv- ative families and individuals, who are not devoted to the relics of their cultural tradit- ion. Many of the magic, acts and healing methods live only in the memories of the older generations, and most of them are preserved by the women rather than the men. Many gypsies now prefer modern medical treatment ; they have a high estimation of X-rays,</page><page sequence="5">Miscellanea 241 but do not appreciate electrocardiographs or injections, and many are still afraid of vaccinations ordered by the authorities. Native Plough in Egypt (Mohamed Riad). - The Egyptian plough which is very simple is used to break the soil and not to invert it. The two basic plough types in Egypt</page><page sequence="7">Miscellanea 243 are the curved beamed type of the Eastern Delta and the double handled type of the southern parts ot Upper Egypt. By combinations of these two at least seven types arose, each within a limited geographic area (but partly coexisting also in the same regions). As to the evolution, it can be concluded that throughout Dynastic Egypt the double handled, straight beamed plough persisted all over Egypt. At the beginning of the Greco- Roman period the Mediterranean (single handled, curved beamed) plough extended over many parts of the Delta and the northern part of Upper Egypt, but did not expel the older double handled plough. The present seven types are the result of these reciprocal influences. The Mediterranean type is widely spread in North Africa and the Middle East. (Bulletin de la Société de Géographie d'Egypte [Le Caire] 33. 1960, pp. 241-277.) The Masters of the Animals among the Wind River Shoshoni (Central U.S. America) (Ake Hultkrantz). - The Wind River Shoshoni reveal in their folklore traces of a former belief in guardians of the game. Thus, mythological beings, and particularly Coyote, show certain characteristics which place them in the category of master animals. The most probable interpretation is that Coyote, in the distant past when the present- day Wind River Shoshoni were carriers of a Basin-like culture, was the lord of all animals, a position he since has lost in the actual religion. There might have been other spiritual rulers of the different species ; the antelope rites seem to indicate that at least the ante- lopes may have had a guardian. On the whole, however, the animal-guardian idea for the different species was very little developed, no doubt due to the scarcity of game in the Basin area from which the Eastern Shoshoni once emerged, and to the consequently little interest in animals as food resources. When the influx of the Plains culture elements started to penetrate aspects of the Shoshoni culture two religious conceptions of the Plains Indians were imported, in all probability along with the Sun Dance : buffalo and eagle as masters of the land animals and the birds, respectively. These conceptions were associated with the dualistic world pattern of some leading Plains tribes. The Shoshoni who had changed a poor game country for plains rich in buffalo easily accepted the idea of the buffalo spirit as the lord of all animals, although they did not develop the master-spirit idea too far : it had only vaguely been associated with the hunting complex in the past and therefore lacked the strength it had attained in other primitive cultures. Nevertheless, Coyote's animal-guardian qualities were soon forgotten, except in the con- servative mythology, at the same time as the buffalo in many ways appeared as a new culture hero. Somehow a compromise between the two spirits came about, for one belongs to mythology, the other to the Sun Dance religion. (Ethnos [Stockholm] 26. 1961, pp. 198-218.)
