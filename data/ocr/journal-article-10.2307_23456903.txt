בחשבון את מידת ההקטנה הצפוייה בהדפסה הסופית, במיוחד בבל הנוגע לכיתובים המופיעים בתוכניות. קנה־מידה קווי חייב ללוות כל איור. כל האיורים(לרבות גראפים) ישולבו בטקסט בסיפרור רצוף. יש לציין על כל איור את מספרו, וכן את שם המאמר והמחבר, ולסמן בטקסט את מיקומו המשוער. יש לצרף רשימה נפרדת של כיתובי האיורים, בהם יצויין גם קנה־המידה של האיור, במקרה שאינו מופיע באיור עצמו. אם משולבות טבלאות בטקסט, עליהן להיות פשוטות בבל האפשר, עם בותרת קצרה והסברים בשוליים, במידת הצורך. גרף המאמר כותרות כותרות פרקי המאמר יותאמו לחלוקה של עד שלוש רמות, כדלהלן: 1. כותרת מרכזית, מודגשת, לפרקים עיקריים. 2. כותרת ימנית מודגשת, בשורה נפרדת, לפרקי מישנה. ג. כותרת ימנית מודגשת, בשורה אחת עם הטקסט, לתת־פרקים (כגון תיאורי טיפוסי קיראמיקה). הפניות והערות שוליים הפניות תשולבנה בטקסט, בשיטה הדומה לזו הנהוגה בבתבי־עת מתחום מדעי החברה. ההפניה המשולבת תבלול את שם המשפחה של המחבר ושנת פירסום המאמר או הספר, ואת מספרי העמודים ו/או האיורים המובאים. פרטים ביבליוגראפיים יימסרו בביבליוגראפיה(ר' להלן). את הערות השוליים יש לצמצם למינימום ההכרחי. אין ההערות מיועדות לפיתוח ריון שאינו קשור ישירות לנושא המאמר, או להבאת מידע חשוב שמקומו בגוף המאמר. ההערות ירוכזו בסוף המאמר, ברף נפרד.</page><page sequence="7">דוגמאות להפניות: Finds from the burial cave.</page><page sequence="20">Guide for Contributors to 'Atiqot 'Atiqot is the journal of the Israel Antiquities Authority. Its purpose is to publish articles dealing with excavations or materials under its authority or full reports on sites excavated on its behalf. Only manuscripts dealing with primary material or directly related to such material will be accepted. General Information Manuscripts submitted for publication in 'Atiqol must consist of a complete, clean copy. They must be typewrit ten in English, double-spaced, on one side only, on good quality, standard-sized paper, with ample (3 cm) margins on all sides. For those using word processors, a high quality printer must be used, and continuous paper must be separated. 'Atiqot is currently being produced on Wordmill (ver. 4.18 and up) on an IBM-type computer; please send a compatible copy of the disk along with the hard copy of the article. Notes, references, and the captions for all figures (including their scales) should be typed, double-spaced, on separate pages, respectively. Figures, black and white drawings or high quality reproductions, should be on good quality paper. 'Atiqot can reduce plates to the appropriate size, but please note that the maximum final size of a pottery plate is 14 x 20 cm. Photographs should be of high quality black and white on glossy paper and can be either 13 x 18 cm or 9 x 12 cm in size. Color photographs should be submitted only on request of the editors. Scale should be included on all drawings and is highly desirable on photographs, especially of objects. Author's name, fig. number and top of photo should be indicated on the back of each illustration. As all illustrations will appear within the running text, both drawings and photographs should be numbered sequentially in the order in which they will appear, and their location should be indicated in the text. All illustrations will be returned to the author. The author will receive the edited copy for authorization of the final version. There is no set policy on the number of offprints sent to the author. The Text Format Headings for subdivisions of the article should follow a three-level format, if needed, as follows: 1. Centered, all capitals, for major divisions. 2. Left justified, italics, first letter of each word capitalized, on separate lines, for important subdivisions. 3. Left justified, italics, first letter of each word capitalized, followed by a full stop, on same line as following text, for items such as pottery types. (Use pages 2-3 of this section as a guide for headings). Citations and Footnotes Both citations and footnotes may be employed. The method of citation is that generally used in the social sciences. Works are cited in the text, enclosed in parentheses. The elements are: author's surname, date of publication and pages (and/or Figs., Pis., etc.). Full bibliographic details are presented in the References. Footnotes, which should be kept to a minimum, may be used to present details supplemental to the information appearing in the text but not to present arguments that belong in the body of the paper. Note: all citations referred to in the body of the text or in the footnotes must appear in full in the References at the end of the article. Examples of citations in the text: 1. Ref. to pages (Epstein 1985:20-22) 2. Ref. to complete work (Epstein 1985) 3. Ref. to different pages, figures and plates (Epstein 1985:22, Fig. 4:1-4, Pl. VIII) 4. Ref. when author is mentioned in the same sentence of the text (1985:22) 5. Ref. to publication just mentioned in the same paragraph (Epstein 1985:22), i.e. repeat the citation.</page><page sequence="21">6. Réf. to two works by the same author (Epstein 1982; 1985) 7. Chronological periods should be written out in full the First time they appear, and thereafter abbreviated, as: Early Bronze Age I-IV EB I-IV Middle Bronze Age I, IIA, I1B MB I, IIA, IIB Late Bronze Age I, II LB I, II Iron Age I, II Iron I, II Periods should not be abbreviated when there are no sub-divisions, e.g. Early Bronze Age. Transliteration Site names are transliterated according to The Official Gazette: A List of Historical Sites, Government Printing Office, Jerusalem, 1960 and supplements. References References should be listed alphabetically by surname, then date of publication. If there is more than one work by the same author, the works should be listed chronologically, earliest first. If more than one work by this author appeared in a single year, the works should be listed alphabetically (beginning with the first main word, i.e. not "a" or "the") by title and the year indicated as a, b, c, etc. If the same author is cited with (a) joint author(s), all the works individually written should be listed first.
