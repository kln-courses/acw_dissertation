<plain_text><page sequence="1">REVIEW OF BOOKS 469 Johann Maier, Studien zur judischen Bibel und ihrer Geschichte (Studia Judaica 28), de Gruyter, Berlin-New York 2004, Leinen, xii, 512 pp., € 128 / sFr 205, ISBN 3-11-018209-2. This is a collection of nineteen previous published (1980-2002) articles of Johann Maier about the Hebrew Bible and its Jewish history of interpreta tion. The articles are revised, actualized as far as the bibliography is con cerned, and unified with regard to the editorial style. In this sense they are not completely identical with their original publication. In two cases (§3; §4) the title has slightly changed, in two other cases the original Italian publica tion has been translated into German (§16; §19). With the exception of two articles that are written in English (§3; §8), all articles are published in German. At the one hand, the articles are concerned with the origin of the "Bible" and its function in Judaism, at the other the articles deal with texts and concep tions that became of importance for the identity of Judaism. Part A ("Torah, Propheten und Schriften (TNK), 'Kanon' und Exegese in der judische Dberlieferung," pp. 1 -136) contains five articles with regard to the Bible in general and the Jewish interpretation of it. They deal with Jewish exegesis (§1: "Moglichkeiten und Formen theologischer Exegese im Judentum"), with the biblical canon in the light of Qumran (§2: "Zur Frage des biblischen Kanons im Friihjudentum"), with biblical interpretation in Qumran (§3: "Biblical Interpretation in the Qumran Literature"), with Jewish Law in early Jewish literature (§4: "Pentateuch, Torah und Recht zwischen Qumran und Sep tuaginta"), and with Flavius Josephus (§5: "Die biblische Geschichte des Flavius Josephus"). Part Β ("Zu Pentateuchtexten," pp. 137-325) contains seven articles about the exegesis of some specific biblical passages and themes from the Pentateuch. Two of them deal with the Japhetites (Gen 10:2-4), one about their traditions in early Jewish literature (§6: "Zur ethnographisch-geographischen tJberliefer ung liber Japhetiten (Gen 10,2-4) im friihen Judentum") and one about their traditions in rabbinic literature (§7: "Zur ethnographisch-geographischen tJber lieferung iiber Japhetiten (Gen 10,2-4) im rabbinischen Judentum"). The other articles deal with the interpretation of Amalek in Josephus (§8: "Amalek in the Writings of Josephus"), with the anthropomorphic problem in mediaeval Judaism concerning the writing possibilities of God (§9: "Der Finger Gottes und der Dekalog. Ein exegetisch-theologisches Problem im mittelalterlichen Judentum"), with the prohibition to return to Egypt (§10: "Grundlage und Anwendung des Verbots der Ruckkehr nach Agypten"), and with the interpretation of Lev 19:16 (§11: "Verleumder oder Verrater. Zur judischen Auslegungsgeschichte von Lev 19,16") and Deut 2:1-8 (§12: "Israel und 'Edom' in den Ausdeutungen zu Dt 2,1-8").
