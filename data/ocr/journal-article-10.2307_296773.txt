Its scope embraces the history, art and archaeology of Rome, Italy and the Roman Empire, down to about A.D. 700. Mediaeval and Renaissance Italy have been left to other bodies already engaged on these subjects. THE JOURNAL OF ROMAN STUDIES The Journal of Roman Studies (11 x 7' inches) is published by the Society in half-yearly parts, and is issued gratuitously and post free to all subscribing members. The Joumnal is sold to non-subscribers at the rate of 30/- a volume or 15/- a part. An arrangement has been effected between the Roman Society, the Hellenic Society and the Classical Association by which the Journals of the three bodies will be conducted in harmony and will occupy distinct provinces of learning. THE LIBRARY AND COLLECTIONS In connexion with the Hellenic Society, the Roman Society maintains at 50 Bedford Square a joint library of Greek and Roman art, archaeology and history, and a collection of lantern-slides and photographs. These are open to members of both Societies. Members of the Roman Society are entitled in respect of their member- ship to borrow three books at a time, and to borrow lantern slides at a charge of 2d. each. Both books and slides can be sent by post. All communications respecting books and lantern slides should be made to the Librarian at 50 Bedford Square, London, W.C.1. MEETINGS By permission of the Society of Antiquaries, afternoon meetings for the reading and discussion of papers on subjects falling within the scope of the Society are held at Burlington House, Piccadilly, W.1. MEMBERSHIP AND SUBSCRIPTIONS The Society consists of life members, annual members, student- associates, subscribing libraries and institutions. The life-membership composition for persons over fifty years of age is ten guineas, for others fifteen guineas. The annual subscription for members is one guinea, for student-associates 1Os. 6d. There is at present no entrance fee. Those who are desirous of joining the Society are invited to communi- cate with the Secretary and Editor at the Haverfield Library, Ashmolean Museum, Oxford.</page></plain_text>
