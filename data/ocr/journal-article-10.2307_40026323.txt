Please note, however, that the Editors do not solicit contributions, nor can they consider proposals for articles which are as yet unwritten, or unfinished work. - Submissions must be original and not previously published or due to be published elsewhere, whether in greater or lesser detail or in the same or another language. - All submissions should be sent as printouts on paper, with text and notes printed in double spacing on one side of the paper only. Each page should be numbered and should bear either the author's surname or a key word of the title. - The Editors also require photocopies of any illustrations, together with a list of numbered captions. - For subsequent return of a printout please enclose postage stamps or international postage reply coupons. It is always assumed, however, that authors have retained an appropriate copy of their work. Conditions of publication in the Journal - Acceptance of a contribution for publication in the Journal implies formal transfer of copyright including electronic rights in that contribution to the Warburg Institute for publication in the Journal or any reprint thereof. The Editors will normally grant permission to an author, author's agent or executors, for a contribution to be reprinted elsewhere after its appearance in the Journal - Authors are not paid but will receive 23 or 24 offprints free of charge, or in the case of joint authors 12 offprints each free of charge (as specified by contract), and may order an additional quantity at their own expense. Any offprints promised in return for favours granted or in lieu of copyright fees must come out of the author's own allowance. Authors of Articles but not of Notes will receive a complimentary copy of the volume. - Submissions which have been accepted for publication by the Editors should be put into Journal house style (see below) and supplied in electronic format. - It is the author's responsibility to provide suitable illustrations for publication. Good-quality black-and-white photographs (glossy finish if possible) or drawings made directly from the work to be reproduced, or else professionally scanned images, are essential. Photographs or scans taken from modern books are not suitable. (Authors who will have no further need of their photographs after the paper is published may like to consider donating them to the photographic collection of either the Warburg or Courtauld Institute.) - The exact provenance of each illustration, with acknowledgement of the right of reproduction, must be provided. It is the author's responsibility to obtain permission to reproduce illustrations, where necessary, and to pay any copyright fees. Charges vary, but it is generally worth stressing that your paper is to appear in a scholarly journal and not for financial profit. Please let us know if any special wording is required by the copyright holders. Names Personal names should be given as well as surnames at first mention (with a few obvious exceptions, e.g. Dante, Einstein, Luther, Michelangelo, Petrarch, Shakespeare). - Use a single space between initials in names, e.g. A. Short quotations within the text should be given single quotation marks and any quotations within them should be given double quotation marks.
