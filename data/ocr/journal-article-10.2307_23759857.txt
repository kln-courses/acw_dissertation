Since the discovery of the ancient Maya city of Naranjo in the northeastern part of the Guatema lan Department of Peten in 1905 a total of 42 carved stelae with and without hieroglyphic inscriptions have been re ported from this major site by various researchers. In early 2001 a team of the Guatemalan archaeologists of the Proyecto Nacional de Antropologi'a e Historia (IDAEH) has recovered the latest carved monument, a stela fragment designated as Naranjo Stela 42, and transferred it to the IDAEH camp at Lake Yaxha in order to protect it (see mexicon, Vol. XXIII, Nr. 4:78-79, 2002). Also in 2001, another stela fragment with a low-relief on the front surface was found in the section of a recently looted area at Naranjo. This broken limestone sculpture is 61 cm high, 74 cm wide, 25 cm thick, and has a maximum relief depth of 0.5 cm.The monument has been designated as Naranjo Stela 43 and also has been removed from its dis turbed finding place in Naranjo and has been brought to Yaxha where it is now in a protected warehouse of the IDAEH, together with Naranjo Stela 42. The fragmentary monument represents the bottom part of a stela with an incomplete plain butt and above showing parts of legs and feet of a standing anthropomorphic figure in a typical Late Classic period style, with the feet turned outwards (Figs. 6, 7).
