THE DIVINE VERDICT OF LEVITICUS X 3 The verse "I will show myself holy ('qds) in those who are near to me (bqrby), and before all the people I will be glorified ('kbd)" is generally interpreted as describing the mode of divine action and not as a justification of divine retribution.1 In this study I shall adopt a different reading for the verse which modifies its inter- pretation. The parallel verbs of the verse appear in the same form in Ezek. xxviii 22: "Behold, I am against you, O Sidon, and I will show my glory (nkbdty) in the midst of you. And they shall know that I am the Lord when I execute judgements in her, and manifest my holiness (nqdsty) in her." However, the theme which underlies the prophecy is not only that God's holiness and glory are sublime Vetus Testamentum XXIX, 1 (1989) 91</page><page sequence="2">SHORT NOTES when he imposes his judgement (as in Isa. v 16), but also that awareness of God will be heightened through the very form of the punishment (as specified in v. 23). The verbs kbd and qds are used as synonyms for divine retribution.2 This meaning is confirmed by the use of the verbs in other contexts. Thus for kbd we read in Ex. xiv 4: "... I will show my glory ('kbdh) over Pharaoh and all his host, and the Egyptians shall know that I am the Lord", and like- wise in vv. 17 and 18 (bhkbdy).3 For qds we read in Lev. xxii 32: "And you shall not profane my holy name lest I show my holiness (nqdsty) among the people of Israel",4 and in Num. xx 12-13: "Because you did not believe in me, to sanctify me in the eyes of the people of Israel... and he showed himself holy (wyqds) in them. " This interpretation of Ezek. xxviii 22 can also be applied to the parallel verse of Lev. x 3. Let us begin with the word bqrby. The Masoretic text points the word with a holam (biqrobay), i.e. "those who are near to me." The priests are so designated in Ezek. xlii 13: " ...the priests who are near to the Lord will eat the most holy offer- ing", and in xliii 19: "...you will give to the Levitical priests of the family of Zadok, who are near to me, to minister to me." Against this reading, however, is the fact that the Priestly source in the Pen- tateuch does not use this expression. This is especially noticeable in the context of the passage under discussion, in a verse characterized by the avoidance of anthropomorphism; compare v. 2: "And fire came forth from before the Lord and devoured them, and they died before the Lord", with the style in the similar case of 2 Sam. vi 7: "And the anger of the Lord was kindled against Uzzah, and God smote him there because he put his hand to the ark, and he died there beside the ark of God."5 A further point is that it seems unlikely that such an honorific epithet should be applied to the priests in a situation where they are being condemned for their sin. It is possible to point the adjective differently, namely with a sere (biqrebay), i.e. "those who approach me."6 The verb qrb is used throughout the Priestly source of the Pentateuch to indicate access to the service at the sanctuary. It is also used in Ezek. xl 46: "...these are the sons of Zadok, who alone among the sons of Levi who approach to the Lord to minister to him," and in xlv 4: "...it will be for the priests who appraoch to minister to him." There is no reason why the term should not be read in the same way in xlii 13 and xliii 19.7 92</page><page sequence="3">SHORT NOTES It is for good reason that Lev. x 3 uses the same term in the con- text of condemning Nadab and Abihu, for it also refers to acts of trespass upon the temple service.8 The rule in such cases was that the offender was subject to the death penalty by the divine hand. The act of Nadab and Abihu was most probably an offence of this type, as may indeed be inferred from Lev. xvi 1: "...when they ap- proached (bqrbtm) before the Lord and died," which refers to the same incident.9 They would therefore have been punished in the same manner. In my view, the verb 'qds here as in Ezek. xxviii 22, is to be interpreted in the light of Lev. xxii 32 and Num. xx 13, which likewise refer to this form of divine punishement. To return to the passage in Lev. x, after God applied the above rule to Nadab and Abihu in v. 2, v. 3 represents the declaration of the verdict. Hence Moses presents it in the first person: "Then Moses said to Aaron: That is what the Lord has said.10 I will show myself holy, etc." A literal translation of the continuation of the verse would be: "In those who trespass upon me I will show myself holy", i.e. by means of the retribution which is inflicted directly and immediately upon a trespasser in the sanctuary. My interpretation also serves to explain the second part of the parallelism in the verse. According to the rules of Num. xviii 1-7 the priests are responsible for the sins of non-priests that consist of trespassing upon the precincts of the tabernacle. Hence if they do not act to kill the trespasser they themselves will bear the punish- ment, in order that divine wrath should not fall upon the communi- ty: "And no stranger shall come near you, but you shall attend to the duties of the sanctuary and the duties of the altar, that there be wrath no more upon the people of Israel" (4-5).11 But when priests themselves unlawfully enter in order to serve at the temple the whole community is also to be punished until the offender has been eliminated.12 Thus if the Lord had not executed Nadab and Abihu the whole community would have suffered from the wrath of his holiness.13 This is precisely the significance of the second part of God's ver- dict: "...and upon all the people I will be glorified (kbbd)."14 The verb kbd has the same meaning here as we suggested for it in the parallel verse of Ezek. xxviii 22 (in the light of Ex. xiv 4, 17, 18), namely the glory of divine retribution, by his wrath. Thus the verdict in this verse corresponds to that in the com- mand given in verse 6: "Do not let the hair of your heads hang 93</page><page sequence="4">SHORT NOTES loose and do not rend your clothes, lest you die, and lest wrath come upon all the congregation.'15 If the priests do not obey the rules of holiness they shall die and all the community shall suffer his holy wrath.
