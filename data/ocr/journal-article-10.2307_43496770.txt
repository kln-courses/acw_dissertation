Seed qualitative characters appear very useful at the supraspecific level in the subtribe Orchidinae. Based on our observations, the sculpturing of the periclinal walls of the medial testa cells, the seed shape and several features of the anticlinal walls of the apical cells showed variability between the studied taxa, with special relevance of the first character. According to this factor, we found one type of seed for Anacamptis and two for Orchis. The taxonomie value of seed coat characters is compared with the last taxonomie proposals for the genera Anacamptis and Orchis. Around this time, several species were separated into distinct genera. Also, in many guides and floras (Sundermann 1980; Soó 1980; Buttler 1991; Bolòs and Vigo 2001; Delforge 2006), the number of Orchis taxa varies considerably, including species that belong to dif- ferent genera, such as Dactylorhiza Neck, ex Nevski, Aceras R. Br., Neotinea Rchb. f., Vermeulenia A. &amp; D. Löve and Anacamptis Rich. Until 1997, Anacamptis was traditionally considered as a monotypie genus (Moore 1980a; Sundermann 1980; Buttler 1991), represented by A. pyramidalis (L.) Rich., a variable and widespread Euro-Mediterranean taxon. However, Del Prete et al. (1991) recognized a distinct species from the Maltese islands, A. urvilleana Sommier &amp; Car-Gatto, currently considered within the variability of A. pyramidalis (Delforge 2006; Baumann et al. 2006). The molecular analyses published by Pridgeon et al. (1997) and Bateman et al. (1997) confirmed the polyphy- letic status of the genus Orchis s.l., and many species were placed into the expanded genera Anacamptis and Neotinea. Besides, the monotypie genus Aceras sensu Moore (1980b) is included in Orchis, a proposal confirmed by Aceto et al. (1999), Cozzolino et al. (2001) and Buttler (2001). Later, Bateman et al. (2003) coded more taxa and corroborated the previous results. Klein (2004) proposed a classification of the genus Orchis in four sections, based on morphological and karyological data, and the existence of natural hybrids in the field, including hybrids between the species of the section Platycheilae (Nevski) E. In their monographic study, Kretzschmar et al. (2007) corroborated the molecular results published by Bateman et al. (2003), providing new morphological characters of the lip, the bracts and the stigmatic cavity, for recognizing the genera Anacamptis , Neotinea and Orchis (incl. Aceras). According to these authors, Anacamptis has 11 species (including 20 subspecies), Orchis , 21 species (including 16 subspecies) and Neotinea , 4 species (including 2 subspe- cies). In this study, natural hybrids were found between species of the same genus, but not among the three genera. Furthermore, within the genus Orchis , proven hybrids only fall between taxa of each subgenera ( Orchis and Mascu- lae ), but not between them. These results are consistent with those of Klein (1989, 2004). Tyteca and Klein (2008) proposed a new taxonomie circumscription of Orchis s.l., segregating several species in the new genera Herorchis D. Tyteca and E. Klein, Androrchis D. Tyteca and E. Klein and Odontorchis D. Tyteca and E. Klein. According to these authors, Neotinea and Anacamptis returned to their former mono- typie position with the species N. maculata (Desf.) Stearn and A. pyramidalis (L.) Rich. The genera Herorchis and Odontorchis included the rest of the species of Anacamptis and Neotinea cited, respectively, by Kretzschmar et al. (2007), and the genus Androrchis contained all the species of the genus Orchis, except the group with an anthropomorphic labellum, which is retained in Orchis (including Aceras). Later, Tyteca and Klein (2009) adopted the enlarged genera Anacamptis and Neotinea sensu Bateman et al. ( 1 997, 2003), but reaffirmed the segregated genus Androrchis. Delforge (2009) published a new classification of Orchis s.l. In this new proposal, the author accepts the taxonom- ical position of Orchis and Neotinea sensu Bateman et al. (1997, 2003); however, he did not support the expanded genus Anacamptis, considering this genus as monotypie (A. pyramidalis), and segregating the rest of the species into the genera Herorchis , Vermeulenia , Anteriorchis and the new genus Paludorchis. Previous studies on orchid seeds have demonstrated the diagnostic and phylogenetic value of certain quantitative and qualitative characters (Clifford and Smith 1969; Arditti et al. 1979; Barthlott and Ziegler 1981; Chase and Pippen 1988; Kurzweil 1993). In the preceding papers, Wildhaber (1972) initiated the morphological study of the seeds in the genus Orchis using light microscopy to obtain a key for the species based principally on the morphology and length of the seeds. Barthlott (1976) confirmed the taxonomie value of the periclinal walls. Ziegler (1981) recognized the character- istic seeds of this genus as Orchis- type. Tohda (1983) analyzed the differences in the sculpturing of the testa seeds in some species of Orchis using SEM images and recognized three groups, two with slanting stripes and one with smooth periclinal walls. Mrkvička (1994) analyzed quantitative and qualitative data of European Orchis using light microscopy, revealing a high diversity in the seed coat micromorphology. This study continues our research about seed micro- morphology in the subtribe Orchidinae (Gamarra et al. 2007, 2008, 2010). A detailed analysis of the morpholo- gical structure of seeds greatly increases our knowledge of individual species and may be helpful for better under- standing of the phylogeny of the taxa examined. The present work aims to survey the seed coat micromorphology of the genera Anacamptis and Orchis sensu Bateman et al. (2003) and Kretzschmar et al. (2007) using SEM and light microscopy, and provides new insights into its potential taxonomie value. Our primary objectives are to describe the seed micromorphology of the studied taxa, identify the principal seed coat characters and incorporate a complementary viewpoint in the systematics of this subtribe. Materials and methods We analyzed seeds of 24 taxa belonging to the genera Anacamptis and Orchis. A list of voucher specimens and localities is given in Appendix I. Seeds were obtained from mature dehiscing capsules collected in the field or from herbarium specimens (MA, MAF, W, according to Holmgren et al. 1990). Fresh seeds were dried for at least 1 month and stored in small envelopes. The samples were mounted on SEM stubs and coated with gold in a sputter-coater (SEM Coating System, Bio- Rad SC 502). Results In Table 1, quantitative data show the means and standard deviation of length and width of the seed, the ratio length/ width, and the number of cells along the longitudinal axis for each population. These data reflected an overlap of values between the species of the same genus, and between different genera, and did not allow discrimination between groups. As shown in Table 2, the principal variations were found in the morphological features of the seed, such as shape, testa cells, presence/absence of sculpturing in the periclinal walls, and the morphology and number of cells in the chalazal end. In the analyzed species, seed shape varied from fusiform (Fig. la, b) to davate (Fig. lc). In some species with fusiform shape, the medial region is wider than the ends, owing to the position of the embryo. The chalazal pole cells varied in number and in several features of the anti- clinal walls. The basal cells were short and polygonal (Fig. Id) and the medial cells elongated (Fig. le). All the seeds showed a sunken line in the longitudinal anticlinal walls (Fig. If) and a slight membrane covering the trans- verse anticlinal walls. Sculptured periclinal walls with thin and slanting ridges are found in the species with anthropomorphic labella, more visible in O. anthropophora (Fig. 4a) and O. italica (Fig. 4b), and weakly in O. mili- taris (Fig. 4c) and O . purpurea (Fig. 4d). In all the analyzed taxa, the color of the seed coat is golden brown, and slightly clear in some species. Only in O. pallens , the anticlinal walls are darker than the rest of the species. Discussion Through our research in the genera of subtribe Orchidinae (Gamarra et al. 2007, 2008, 2010, 201 1), we confirmed that seeds display constant characters that can be useful for taxonomy, which are highly consistent with previous molecular, genetic and morphological data. Our results revealed the correlation between genera and the seed coat micromorphology, finding a type for each studied genus, with special relevance of qualitative characters as the sculpturing of the periclinal walls of the medial testa cells, the seed shape and the anticlinal walls of the chalazal pole. Based on our observations, we generally confirmed a great concordance between the seed coat micromorphology and the molecular analysis published by Bateman et al. However, despite molecular evidence for close relationships of cer- tain taxa, we found micromorphological data that are not congruent with the molecular analyses, e.g., in the genus Platanthera (Gamarra et al. 2008) or in the clade Chamor- chis/Traunsteinera (Gamarra et al. 201 1). In Anacamptis sensu Bateman et al. (2003), slanting ridges are showed in the periclinal walls, and two groups are recognized, one with thin and dense ridges, and the other one with thick and distant ridges. Slanting ridges were observed by Tohda (1983) in Orchis morio , O. picta , O. coriophora and O. laxiflora. Later, Mrkvička (1994) reported the same ornamentation, but with different thicknesses of ridges in the periclinal walls for Anacamptis pyramidalis , Orchis coriophora , O. morio and O. palustris. Furthermore, our quantitative data (Table 1) agreed with Mrkvička (1994) and Arditti and Ghani (2000). These results are largely congruent with the monophyly obtained by Bateman et al. (2003), like it was obtained in the genus Neotinea (Gamarra et al. 2007). This proposal is also well supported by karyological and genetic data (Buttler 2001; Kretzschmar et al. 2007). The molecular studies (Bateman et al. 2003) showed different lineages, which Kretzschmar et al. (2007) attributed to sections. Our results perfectly fit with this taxonomie proposal, princi- pally in the correlation between the seed micromorpho- logical patterns and the sections Laxiflorae, Moriones and Coriophorae. However, the seeds of Anacamptis papi- lionacea , A. collina and A. pyramidalis showed few dif- ferences to confirm the sections Papilionaceae, Saccatae and Anacamptis , respectively, but other morphological characters allowed recognizing them. Seeds with slanting ridges in the periclinal walls occur in species with anthropomorphic labella, an ornamentation observed by Tohda (1983) in O. longicruńs (=0. italica) and O. purpurea , and Mrkvička (1994) in O. militaris and O. purpurea. The rest of the species lack ornamentation, as was observed by Tohda (1983) in O . mascula and Mrkvička (1994) in O. pallens , O. spitzelii and O. mascula subsp. signifera. Within the genus Orchis , Kretzschmar et al. (2007) included the species with anthropomorphic labella in the subgenus Orchis, which comprised the sections Orchis , Anthropophorae and Italicae. Later, Tyteca and Klein (2008) considered that all these species formed the repre- sentation of this genus. Our results support the inclusion of these species into the genus Orchis , because all the studied seeds showed the same ornamentation pattern, more clearly visible in O. anthropophora and O. italica , which confirms the separation into sections. Quantitative data showed that seed length is greater in O. anthropophora and less in O. italica than in the rest of the species. Our results were consistent with morphological, karyological and genetic data (Cauwet-Marc and Balayer 1 984; Buttler 200 1 ; Klein 2004). The species without anthropomorphic labella were included in the sections Masculae Lindl., Provinciales Pari., Pusillae Pari, and Robustocalcare Hautz. of the subgenus Masculae by Kretzschmar et al. (2007), and in the new genus Androrchis by Tyteca and Klein (2008). The studied seeds of these species show smooth periclinal walls, which according to our research (Gamarra et al. 2010) is consistent with their inclusion in the mentioned genus Androrchis. This proposal is also consistent with the morphological and karyological data (Cauwet-Marc and Balayer 1984; Buttler 2001 ; Klein 2004). As we mentioned above, these results are not congruent with the monophyly obtained in the genus Orchis in Bateman et al. (2003). Within the genus Androrchis, the species of the section Masculae sensu Kretzschmar et al. (2007) showed davate seeds with highly undulate anticlinal walls in the chalazal end, which confirmed the segregation of this group of the rest of the genus, constituting the section Androrchis. On the other hand, the rest of the analyzed species (Androrchis provincialis, A. pallens , A. quadripunctata , A. spitzelii , A. cazorlensis) showed seeds with straight to sinuous anticlinal walls in the chalazal end.
