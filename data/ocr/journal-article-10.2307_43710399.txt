Although this book has been in circulation for several years, it is still fitting to call attention to its many good points and its great practicality for the student of Sacred Scripture. "Multum in parvo" is a phrase that sums up this miniature encyclopedia of the Bible. There are many dozens of articles. They are written in French by Von Allmen and thirty-six scholarly collaborators, and are all signed. Some articles are no more than a column or two; generally, they cover several pages of fine print. Long or short, they deal adequately with the key words and ideas of Old and New Testaments. In the otherwise fine article on "Pardon" there is an over-simplification re- garding the prophetical attitude towards external sacrifice; and no allowance at all is made for the acts of a sinner with regard to forgiveness. The article "Ministère" by P. H. Menoud is enlightening. He considers the Pastoral Epistles as post-apostolic: "Elles ont été vraisemblablement rédigées par un disciple de Paul, utilisant peut-être des notes de l'apôtre ou s'inspirant de ses indications orales." Yet he concludes with the statement that the NT Church was, in a word, "une christocratie apostolique." A moderate approach is taken to the question of St. Peter's primacy in the article "Noms propres." Among the more outstanding articles are those on "Nombres" and "Famille (N.T.)," both by Von Allmen. Even lesser treatises are full and informative. One example is the article on "Face." The author, S. Amsler, points out that besides a general scriptural use of the word to designate the visible side of</page><page sequence="2">270 The Catholic Biblical Quarterly [Vol. 19 some reality, such as the face of the earth or fields or waters or heavens, it has a particular meaning when referred to man (the face of man being a mirror of hidden sentiments) or God (by anthropomorphism, the face of God indicating favor or disfavor towards men, and at times being used in lieu of the Person of God). Viewpoints on particular problems are, of course, far too numerous to men- tion. By way of example, we might select the article on "Ange." Concerning the mysterious Maleak Jahweh of the OT, it is A. Lelièvre's view that "Il y a incontestablement dans cette figure une ébauche du mystère de l'Incarnation. D'autre part cet ange est le mode 'accidentel' de la présence et de l'action de Dieu au sein de son peuple." On the other hand, with regard to the angels of the churches in Ap 2-3, M. Bouttier states: "Nous préférons, en rapprochant ce passage de Mat. 18,10 (les anges des enfants), y voir l'expression de la réalité secrète et invisible de notre vie cachée en Dieu. L'apôtre est comme enlevé au ciel et c'est l'autre aspect de la vie de ces Églises qui se révèle à lui et qu'il désigne sous ce terme." Here, then, is a handy one-volume biblical encyclopedia, a welcome aid to the biblical scholar. Eric May, O.F.M. Cap.</page></plain_text>
