<plain_text><page sequence="1">IASIL Bibliography Bulletin for 2004 On behalf of the Bibliography Committee, I am pleased to present another instalment of the Bibliography Bulletin. Readers should be aware that at the AGM in Galway last year it was decided that translations of Irish literary works into other languages would be omitted from the bibliography unless they contain introductions, notes, or critical commentaries. Also, the 'General Studies' section is restricted to general works on Irish literatures, theatre, language, or culture more generally. Trying to embrace the whole field of 'Irish Studies' in a consistent manner for this section has proved impossible to achieve. However, works relating to writers or culture in the Irish diaspora are now included. Perhaps in future they will warrant a separate section. In this Bulletin, the listing of works relating to James Joyce is the largest for any single author in the history of the Bibliography, a reflection of worldwide interest in Joyce during the centenary of Bloomsday. Readers are very welcome to provide feedback on any aspect of the bibliography, especially to alert me to any missing items so that they can be added to next year's listing, or to suggest improvements in layout that might assist them with their research. The membership of the committee continues to grow. This year we welcome Pirkko Hautamaki (Finland), Mary Massoud (Egypt), Carmen Szabo (Romania), and Malcolm Sen (India). A welcome too to Maria Jesus Lorenzo Modia who is now representing Spain and Richard Corballis, New Zealand. Eileen Fausett who has represented the United Kingdom since 1997 is resigning; our thanks to Eileen for her reports over the years. It is hoped that a replacement will be found in the near future. I would welcome further contributors, especially from south-east Asian countries, South and Central America, Africa, and the Middle East. If you could represent a country at present without a representative, or you know of anyone who could, do let me know. And if you live in a country without a representative and are publishing on Irish literature, send me details at the email address below before 31 May 2006.
