It offers a new look on the most influenciai bibliography, regarding the subject, which has been produced from the first part of nineteenth century to our days. Various theories have been proposed about that prob- lem and some scholars have even denied the existence itself of aniconism. Yet the ques- tion still seems to be open. This work calls into question those theories and presents a new interpretation of data both from an art historical and a doctrinal point of view. The main point implied is that anicoism was the solution to a difficult artistic problem. This solution was strictly bound with the idea of the Buddha which those early artis- tic works intended to give.
