Stuart Carroll, Blood and Violence in Early Modern France, OUP, xiii + 369 pp., uses the term 'vindicatory violence to encompass acts of violence such as revenge killing and the duel, which repair an honour or injury and which are suggestive of a reciprocal relationship between the parties'.
