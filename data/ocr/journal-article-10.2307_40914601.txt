In this essay I argue that Joseph Albo was the first medieval Jewish philosopher actually to attempt an axiomatic presentation of Judaism because while he accepted the Maimonidean thesis that the Torah was a deductive science, he rejected the thesis that the content of that science was physics and metaphysics.
