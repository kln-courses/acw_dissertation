INFORMATION FOR AUTHORS THE AMERICAN ANTHROPOLOGIST publishes original articles in the entire field of an- thropology whether or not the author is a member of the American Anthropological As- sociation. Contributions should be typewritten on standard 81/2 x 11 inch paper, double spaced, and on one side of the paper only. Footnote references and bibliographies should follow standard form. Footnote references should be by author and date only, as: MEAD, 1939, p. 200. Works cited are to be placed in a bibliography at the end of the article, in alphabetical and calendrical order, as, for books: LINTON, RALPH 1945 The Cultural Background of Personality. D. Appleton Century Co., New York. For citations from journals: MEAD, MARGARET 1939 Native Languages as Field-work Tools. American Anthropologist, n.s., Vol. 41, No. 2, pp. 189-205. Galley proof only will be sent to authors, and lengthy or unusual changes therein may be charged to the author. Illustrations must be kept to a minimum or paid for.
